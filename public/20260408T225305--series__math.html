1. Series

A series can be represented in the form \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}a_n\)

1.1. Types of Series

1.1.1. Harmonic Series

\(\sum^\infty_{n=1}\frac{1}{n}\)

NOTE: This series is always divergent!

1.1.2. Telescoping Series

\(\sum^\infty_{n=1}(\frac{1}{n}-\frac{1}{n+1})\)

1.1.3. Geometric Series

\(\sum^\infty_{n=1}ar^{n-1}\)

Value of convergence can be found with the formula: \(\frac{a}{1-r}\)

  • Converges if : \(|r| < 1\)
  • Diverges if : \(|r| \geq 1\)

1.1.4. P-Series

\(\sum^\infty_{n=1}\frac{1}{n^p}\)

  • Converges if : \(p < 1\)
  • Diverges if : \(p \geq 1\)
