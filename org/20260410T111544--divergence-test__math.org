* Divergence Test

** Theorem

If the series \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}a_n\) is convergent, then \(\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}a_n=0\).

** Corollary (Test for Divergence)

If \(\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}a_n\) does not exist of if \(\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}a_n \neq 0\),
then the series \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}a_n\) is divergent.

** Theorem

If \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}a_n\) and \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}b_n\) are convergent series, then so are \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}ca_n\)
(where \(c\) is a constant),

\(\sum^\infty_{n=1}(a_n+b_n)\), and \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}(a_n-b_n)\), and

- 

\(\sum^\infty_{n=1}(a_n \pm b_n)=\sum^\infty_{n=1}a_n \pm \sum^\infty_{n=1}b_n\)
- \(\sum^\infty_{n=1}ca_n=c\sum^\infty_{n=1}a_n\)
