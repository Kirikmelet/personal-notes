* Alternating Series Tests
 
** Theorem (Alternating Series Test)

If the alternating series
\(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n-1} a_n\) (where \(a_n > 0\)) satisfies the
following 2 conditions:
 
1. \(a_{n+1} \leq a_n\) for all \(n\).
 
2. \(\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 0\)
 
   Then the series is convergent.
 
** Theorem (Error)

If \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n-1} a_n = S\) and
\(a_{n+1} \leq a_n\) for all \(n\) and \(\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 0\), then
\(|S - S_n| \leq a_{n+1}\).
 
/Definition/ A series \(\sum a_n\) is called absolutely convergent if
the series \(\sum |a_n|\) is convergent.
 
A series \(\sum a_n\) is called absolutely divergent if the series
\(\sum |a_n|\) is divergent.
 
A series \(\sum a_n\) is called conditionally convergent if it is not
absolutely convergent and it converges on its own.
