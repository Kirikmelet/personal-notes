* Convergence Test


** Theorem

If the series \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} a_n\) is convergent, then \(\lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n = 0\)

** Corollary Theorem (Test for Divergence)

If \(\lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n \ne 0\) then the series \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} a_n\) must diverge.

** Theorem

if \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} a_n\) and \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} b_n\) are convergent series, then the series \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} can_n\) (\(c\) is a constant) and \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} (a_n\pm b_n)\) are also convergent.

- \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} (a_n \pm b_n)\) = \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} a_n \pm \sum^{\infty}_{n=1} b_n\)
- \(\sum^{\infty}_{n=1} can_n = c \cdot \sum^{\infty}_{n=1}a_n\) (This can be used with divergent series.)

** Theorem

Convergence and Divergence of a series is /not/ changed by deleting or adding a finite number of terms from the series.

If we have \(\sum^{\infty}_{k=1} a_k\) and \(\sum^{\infty}_{k=10} a_k\) and \(5+\sum^{\infty}_{k=1} a_k\), then all converge or all diverge.
